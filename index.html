Frequently asked questions

AI search optimization — also called Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) — is the process of making your business visible in AI-generated search answers from platforms like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Claude, and Grok. Unlike traditional SEO, which focuses on Google's link-based algorithm, GEO optimizes your content to be cited, quoted, and recommended by large language models. According to Princeton University's KDD 2024 research, systematic GEO optimization can improve AI citation visibility by up to 40%.
To appear in ChatGPT results, your business needs strong AI citation signals: factual content with specific data points, presence on third-party platforms AI trusts (directories, review sites, industry publications), proper schema markup, and an llms.txt file. RankForward's audit tests your business against 20+ prompts and identifies exactly where you're invisible.
GEO is a methodology developed through academic research at Princeton University, published at ACM KDD 2024. It identifies 9 specific content optimization methods that increase how often AI search engines cite a source. GEO is different from traditional SEO: SEO helps you get found on Google. GEO gets you cited by AI.
Traditional SEO ranks you in Google's blue links using backlinks and keyword optimization. GEO gets you cited in AI-generated answers using factual density, authority signals, and third-party citations. A business can rank #1 on Google and score 0% in AI answers — the ranking factors are completely different.
